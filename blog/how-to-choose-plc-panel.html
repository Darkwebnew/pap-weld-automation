PLC Automation Guide

How to Choose the
Right PLC Panel

A step-by-step guide for factory owners and production managers buying a PLC control panel for the first time — or upgrading an existing one.

Published by PAP Weld Core Automation Engineering Team

Step 1 — Define Your I/O Requirements

I/O stands for Input/Output — the number of signals going into and out of your PLC. Count every sensor, switch, push button (inputs) and every motor, solenoid, actuator, indicator (outputs).

Always add 20–25% spare I/O capacity to your count. This future-proofs the panel for line expansions without requiring a new panel. If you count 40 I/O today, spec a panel for 50–52 I/O minimum.

Step 3 — Specify the Enclosure Rating

The enclosure rating (IP rating) defines how well the panel is protected against dust and water. Your environment determines what you need:

  • IP20 Clean office / control room environment. No water exposure.
  • IP54 Factory floor. Protected against dust and splashing water. Most common for industrial panels.
  • IP65 Washdown environments. Food, beverage, chemical plants. Fully dust-tight and water jet protected.

Step 4 — Decide on Communication Protocol

If your panel needs to talk to a SCADA system, remote HMI, or other machines — you need a communication protocol. Modbus RTU (RS-485) is the simplest and most common in India. For newer Industry 4.0 systems, EtherNet/IP or Profinet is used. Specify this requirement upfront — it affects PLC model selection and panel wiring.

Step 5 — Insist on These Before Accepting the Panel

  • As-built electrical drawings in softcopy (AutoCAD or PDF)
  • PLC program backup file in original software format
  • Cable ferrule markings on every wire
  • Factory Acceptance Test (FAT) before dispatch
  • On-site commissioning and operator training included
