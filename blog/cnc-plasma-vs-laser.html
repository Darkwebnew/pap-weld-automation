CNC Plasma vs
Laser Cutting

Which is the right investment for your factory? A practical comparison for Indian heavy fabrication and structural steel buyers.

Published by PAP Weld Core Automation Engineering Team

The Short Answer

For most Indian heavy fabrication factories cutting mild steel from 3mm to 40mm — CNC plasma is the better investment. It costs significantly less to purchase, costs less to operate, and handles thick material better than most laser systems in the same price range.

Laser cutting makes more sense when you need very fine tolerances on thin material (under 6mm), are cutting stainless or aluminium at high volume, or when edge quality is critical for visible surfaces.

When to Choose CNC Plasma

  • Your primary material is mild steel 6mm to 40mm thickness
  • You need wide cutting widths (2000mm to 6000mm)
  • Budget is under ₹30 lakh for the cutting machine
  • You are in structural steel, shipbuilding, or heavy fabrication
  • You want simple maintenance and local service support

When to Choose Laser

  • Primarily cutting stainless steel or aluminium under 10mm
  • Very high production volume on thin sheet with tight tolerances
  • Premium edge finish is required without secondary grinding
